Mixed Conditionals

"The art of connecting the past to the present (and vice versa)."

Type A: Past Cause → Present Result

You did (or didn't) something in the past, and it affects your current state.

If + Past Perfect, WOULD + Base Form

Example: If I had studied medicine, I would be a doctor now.

Type B: Present Cause → Past Result

Something about your general character or situation affected a past event.

If + Past Simple, WOULD HAVE + Past Participle

Example: If I weren't so shy, I would have invited her to the party last night.

Hypothetical Scenarios

Rewrite the following situations using Mixed Conditionals.

1. Situation: I didn't take your advice yesterday. Now I am in a lot of trouble.

2. Situation: Mark is not very talented. That's why he didn't get the job at the orchestra.

3. Situation: I spent all my money last week. I can't go to the cinema with you today.
