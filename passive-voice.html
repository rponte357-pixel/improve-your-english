Aplicación interactiva para aprender Advanced Passive en inglés

Tipo 1 — Verbos de reporte

Se usan con verbos como say, think, believe, know, expect, report, consider, claim. Hay dos estructuras:

It + be + past participle + that-clause
Subject + be + past participle + to-infinitive
People say she is very talented. ↓ impersonal It is said that she is very talented. ↓ personal She is said to be very talented.
They believed he had escaped. ↓ He was believed to have escaped.
Tipo 2 — Causativa have / get

Expresa que alguien hace que otra persona realice una acción por nosotros.

have + object + past participle
get + object + past participle
Someone repaired my car. (I arranged it) ↓ I had my car repaired.
Someone cut her hair. ↓ She got her hair cut.
Someone is delivering the packages. ↓ We are having the packages delivered.
Tipo 3 — Pasiva con modales

Los modales se combinan con pasiva para expresar obligación, posibilidad o deducción.

modal + be + past participle (presente)
modal + have been + past participle (pasado)
This must be done immediately. The report should have been submitted yesterday. The package might have been stolen. These rules ought to be followed.
Tipo 4 — Pasiva impersonal

Se usa para distanciarse de la información, típico en contextos formales, noticias o académicos.

It is said / known / reported / believed / thought / claimed + that
It is widely known that the economy is recovering. It has been reported that several people were injured. It is claimed that the new policy will create jobs.
Tipo 5 — Infinitivo y gerundio pasivos

El infinitivo y el gerundio también pueden ir en forma pasiva.

to be + past participle (infinitivo pasivo)
being + past participle (gerundio pasivo)
She expects to be promoted next year. He hates being interrupted during meetings. The contract needs to be signed today. I remember being told about this rule.
Tipo 6 — Doble objeto pasivo

Verbos como give, send, show, offer, tell, award, teach pueden tener dos formas pasivas.

Indirect obj. + be + past participle + direct obj.
Direct obj. + be + past participle + to + indirect obj.
They gave her a prize. ↓ forma 1 She was given a prize. ↓ forma 2 A prize was given to her.
